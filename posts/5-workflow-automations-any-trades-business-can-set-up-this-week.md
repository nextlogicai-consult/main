Let me guess. You are great at the actual work. The problem is everything around it.

The missed call you did not get back to in time and lost the job. The quote you sent that just went quiet because you forgot to follow up. The five-star customer who would absolutely leave you a review if someone just asked them at the right moment. The scheduling back-and-forth that somehow takes 45 minutes for a job that takes 2 hours.

If you run a trades business, whether you're a plumber, electrician, HVAC tech, landscaper, or general contractor, this is just the reality of the job. You are running a business with your hands full.

The good news is that most of this can be automated. Not with some expensive enterprise software. With tools that cost less than a tank of gas per month and can be set up in an afternoon.

Here are five that actually work.

## 1. Missed Call Text-Back

This one might be the highest-ROI automation in this entire list.

When a potential customer calls you and you don't pick up, most of them move on to the next contractor on Google within about three minutes. You never even knew they were interested.

A missed call text-back sends an automatic text message the moment you miss a call. Something like: "Hey, sorry I missed you. I'm on a job right now. What can I help you with?" It goes out in seconds, keeps the conversation alive, and captures leads you would have lost entirely.

Tools like GHL (GoHighLevel), Jobber, or even a simple setup through Zapier and Twilio can handle this. Some phone systems have it built in.

Cost: roughly $30 to $50 a month depending on the tool. Time to set up: about two hours including testing.

What changes: you stop losing warm leads to voicemail.

## 2. Automated Quote Follow-Up

You send a quote. You hear nothing. A week goes by. You think about following up but you are on a job, then another job, and by the time you remember it has been three weeks and it feels awkward.

Meanwhile the customer picked someone else, not because your price was wrong, but because the other guy followed up twice and you didn't follow up at all.

An automated quote follow-up sequence fixes this without you lifting a finger. When a quote goes out, a timer starts. If no response comes in 48 hours, an automatic message goes out: "Just checking you received the quote okay. Happy to answer any questions." If still no response after another 48 hours, one more: "Following up one last time. If the timing isn't right, no worries at all."

Then it stops. No more chasing. No awkwardness. And a much higher close rate on the quotes you are already sending.

This can be set up in Jobber, ServiceTitan, or with a basic Zapier workflow connected to your email. If you are sending quotes as PDFs through Gmail, there are tools that track opens and trigger follow-ups automatically.

Cost: often included in whatever job management software you already use. Time to set up: one afternoon.

## 3. Job Completion Review Request

You finish a job. The customer is happy. You pack up and drive to the next one. Three months later you wonder why you only have 12 Google reviews when you have done 200 jobs this year.

The answer is simple. Nobody asked.

An automated review request sends a message to the customer about two hours after a job is marked complete. Something short and genuine: "Really glad we could help today. If you have a minute, a Google review means a lot to a small business like ours." Include the direct link. Make it one tap.

The timing matters. Two hours after completion is when the customer is still thinking about you and the job is fresh. A week later they have moved on.

This automation alone, in our experience with clients, typically doubles or triples the review volume within 60 days. Reviews drive search rankings. Search rankings drive inbound calls. Inbound calls drive revenue. It is one of the clearest ROI chains in local business.

Most job management platforms have this built in. If yours doesn't, a simple Zapier trigger off a job status change into an SMS tool handles it.

## 4. Scheduling Confirmation and Reminder Sequence

Every no-show costs you money. Not just the lost job revenue, but the time you blocked, the drive you made, and the other job you could have taken.

A simple confirmation and reminder sequence cuts no-shows dramatically. Here is what it looks like:

- Booking confirmed: immediate confirmation message with the date, time, and what to expect
- 48 hours before: reminder with the appointment details and a simple "reply YES to confirm or call us to reschedule"
- 2 hours before: final reminder with your name and contact number

If someone does not confirm, you know in advance and can start working the waitlist or filling the slot.

This is not complicated. Most scheduling tools have this built in. If you are still booking jobs over the phone and writing them in a notebook, this is also a good moment to consider moving to a simple digital calendar with automated reminders. Calendly, Jobber, or even Google Calendar with a connected tool can handle this.

Cost: often free or included in existing tools. Time to set up: one to two hours.

## 5. End of Job Follow-Up and Rebooking Prompt

This one is underused and it compounds over time.

Most trades businesses have repeat work available that they never capture. The customer who had their furnace serviced last October and is due again this October. The landscaping client whose gutters need cleaning every spring. The plumber's customer who mentioned they were thinking about a bathroom renovation next year.

An automated follow-up sequence that goes out at the right interval keeps you front of mind without any manual effort.

For recurring services, it is straightforward: set a reminder to go out 11 months after the last service. For project-based work, a simple check-in three to six months later, asking if there is anything else they need or if they know anyone looking for the same work, generates referrals that most contractors never think to ask for.

This is relationship maintenance on autopilot. It feels personal because it is timed around the customer's actual job history, even though it runs automatically.

Tools like Jobber, HouseCall Pro, or a basic CRM connected to an email tool can handle this. The setup takes a few hours but the payoff is years of repeat business you would otherwise leave on the table.

## The Honest Part

None of this is magic and none of it replaces being good at your job. A customer who had a bad experience is not going to leave you a five-star review just because you asked nicely via automation.

You do not need a developer. You do not need to overhaul your whole operation. You need one afternoon, a few cheap tools, and the willingness to set it up properly once so it runs on its own from there.
